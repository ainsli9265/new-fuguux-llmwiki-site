# Design Principles (U.S. Web Design System)

Five core design principles published by USWDS, the U.S. federal government's shared design system, framed as an evaluative checklist for federal digital services under the 21st Century Integrated Digital Experience Act. The first source in this wiki that isn't a chapter of *The Design of Sites*.

## Key points

- **Start with Real User Needs** — product decisions driven by ongoing research with real people, not assumptions. Mostly reconfirms what's already covered in [[know-your-customers]] and [[customer-research-methods]]; not separately expanded here.
- **Earn Trust** — trust is never assumed, only earned and re-earned: clear identity signals, protecting sensitive data, system reliability, and honest, plain-language communication. Promoted to its own page, [[trust-and-credibility]], since no existing page treated trust as a first-class design principle in its own right.
- **Embrace Accessibility** — folded into [[accessibility]], adding named standards (WCAG 2.1, Revised Section 508), keyboard/screen-reader navigability, and multilingual content — dimensions that page didn't have yet, which previously covered only color-vision deficiency and motor-control target sizing. The WCAG/Section 508 material was later split out into its own page, [[wcag]], once two dedicated Wikipedia sources made that standard's structure substantial enough to outgrow a single paragraph on [[accessibility]] — this source's Section 508 contrast point lives there now.
- **Promote Continuity** — folded into [[interface-design-principles]]'s "Be consistent throughout" principle, extending it with cross-device, cross-browser, and cross-session consistency.
- **Listen** — folded into [[web-site-development-process]]'s Maintenance phase, extending its existing metrics/server-log content with bug-reporting channels, dedicated analytics tooling, and publishing metrics back to stakeholders.
