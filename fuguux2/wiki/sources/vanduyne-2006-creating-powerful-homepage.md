# Creating a Powerful Homepage

Pattern Group C of *The Design of Sites* — the first of the book's lettered pattern groups ingested into this wiki. Documents two patterns in full six-part format (background/problem/forces/solution/other-patterns, per [[web-design-patterns]]): [[homepage-portal]] (C1) and [[up-front-value-proposition]] (C2).

## Key points

- [[homepage-portal]] (C1) frames the homepage as the site's single highest-leverage page — seen far more often than any other — and lays out the competing demands (brand, look-and-feel, content, personalization, navigation clarity, download speed) it has to satisfy at once within the limited space above the fold.
- A recurring rule of thumb: dedicate roughly 95% of above-the-fold space/links to the audience subgroup that makes up 95% of visitors, with the remaining 5% (often reached via a role-selection page) for everyone else.
- [[up-front-value-proposition]] (C2) gives a concrete, four-step, customer-tested process (team brainstorm → stakeholder shortlist → customer importance/uniqueness rating → customer pick) for arriving at the one-sentence promise that anchors a homepage — directly extending the "decide on the Web site's value proposition" step named but not detailed back in [[web-site-development-process]]'s Discovery phase.
- Both patterns lean heavily on patterns from other groups already touched on inline elsewhere in this wiki — [[search-action-module]], grid layout, page templates, clear first reads, [[multiple-ways-to-navigate]] — reinforcing the "pattern language" idea from [[web-design-patterns]] that patterns complete and reference each other rather than standing alone.
