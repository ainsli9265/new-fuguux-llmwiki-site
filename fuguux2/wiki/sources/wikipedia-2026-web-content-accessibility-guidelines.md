# Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (Wikipedia)

A dedicated encyclopedia article on WCAG itself — more detailed than the general "Web Accessibility" article ([[wikipedia-2026-web-accessibility]]) on the standard's internal structure, version history, and jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction legal adoption. Wikipedia articles are continuously edited, so `year` above reflects the access date (2026-06-24), not a fixed publication year.

## Key points

- WCAG 2.0 (2008) has exactly 61 testable success criteria under 12 guidelines and 4 POUR principles; confirmed directly against the saved HTML's literal text, not just an AI-summarized paraphrase.
- WCAG 2.1 (2018) adds 17 success criteria (78 total); WCAG 2.2 (October 2023) adds a further 9 (87 total relative to 2.0) and deprecates success criterion 4.1.1 (Parsing).
- WCAG 3.0 has been in public draft since early 2021 (last updated September 2025), with no official Recommendation status yet and an expected departure from the 2.x scoring model.
- Specific legal mandates: U.S. DOJ final rule (April 24, 2024) setting WCAG 2.1 AA for state/local government under ADA Title II; EU Directive 2016/2102 and the European Accessibility Act (binding June 28, 2025); UK public-sector requirement since September 2018; Canada's *Jodhan* Supreme Court decision (2012) reinforcing federal compliance.
