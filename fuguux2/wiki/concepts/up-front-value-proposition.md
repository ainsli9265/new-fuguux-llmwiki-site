# Up-Front Value Proposition

**Problem:** visitors who can't immediately tell what a site or company offers will often leave rather than dig for the answer, and a wrong or absent first impression takes expensive, slow trust-rebuilding to undo later.

**Solution:** state a persuasive, unique promise — what the site offers and why it's worth a visitor's time — as one of the first things read on the [[homepage-portal]], typically right next to the logo so it's read alongside it. The bar (citing David Ogilvy's advertising principle that a persuasively-promised unique benefit gets read and acted on more) is that the statement must be both *persuasive* and *unique*, not simply descriptive.

A repeatable four-step process for arriving at one:

1. **Brainstorm** — get the whole design team in a room (especially its most visionary/vocal members) and generate candidate ten-word-or-fewer promises with no judgment or filtering in the moment.
2. **Shortlist** — have a smaller group of marketing/business stakeholders narrow the brainstormed list to the ten strongest candidates, screened against strategic fit, persuasiveness, and uniqueness.
3. **Customer-rate** — survey a sample of customers (around 100) to rate each of the ten on importance (0–10) and uniqueness (0–10); keep only candidates averaging 7+ on both. If none clears that bar, the brainstorming step needs another pass.
4. **Customer-select** — survey customers again to pick a single winner from the survivors.

**Forces:** a value proposition only works if the site (and the company's offline practices) actually deliver on the promise on every page — stating it well is necessary but not sufficient, and getting it wrong costs disproportionate effort to correct, since it means re-earning trust rather than just rewording a tagline. Once chosen, integrate it tightly with site branding so it reads as a single, fast first impression rather than two competing messages — see [[homepage-portal]]. This pattern fleshes out the "decide on the value proposition" step named in the Discovery phase of [[web-site-development-process]].
