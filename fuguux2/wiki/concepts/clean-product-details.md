# Clean Product Details

The product details page is the decision point where a customer chooses to buy or leave. Most of the design work on this page is about sequencing information: putting what a customer needs to make a purchase decision [[above-the-fold|above the fold]], and everything else below it.

## Above-the-fold elements

The following elements belong [[above-the-fold|above the fold]] — visible without scrolling — because they are the minimum needed to make a purchase decision:

- **Navigation** — category breadcrumb or site navigation so the customer can orient, return to browsing, or compare alternatives without losing the page
- **Product image** — a clear, zoomable thumbnail; for products with variations (color, style), images that update to match the selected option
- **Needs-oriented description** — a short summary written around what the customer wants to accomplish with the product, not what the company wants to say about it; the distinction matters because customers evaluate products against their own tasks, not against a feature list
- **Price** — including any discount from a regular price, and taxes or shipping if they can be known at this stage
- **Options pick list** — size, color, quantity, or other variants; selecting an option should update the image and price where applicable
- **Customer ratings** — a summary (star rating, number of reviews) that signals social proof and links to the full review section below
- **Delivery estimate** — the expected arrival date or time window, visible before the customer commits
- **Add to Cart button** — the primary [[action-buttons|action button]], large and unambiguous; for out-of-stock items, hide or disable this button and show availability status instead; for pre-order items, substitute a "Pre-order" label

## Below-the-fold elements

Below the fold belongs everything useful after the decision has been made or for customers doing deeper research:

- **Full product description** — complete specifications, materials, dimensions, compatibility notes
- **Expert and customer reviews** — the [[recommendation-community]] section (write/preview/post flow, meta-ratings for surfacing trustworthy reviews)
- **Related products** — [[cross-selling-and-up-selling|cross-sells and up-sells]] relevant to this product; accessories, complementary items, higher-tier alternatives
- **Product comparator** — a side-by-side table if the site carries multiple variants or competing items in the same category

## Standardized templates

Product pages benefit from standardized templates per product category, not universal templates across all products. What "above the fold" means for a book (author, edition, ISBN, page count) differs from what it means for clothing (size chart, fabric, care instructions) or electronics (compatibility, technical specs). A template built for the product category ensures the right fields appear in the right order for every product in that group, without requiring custom layout work on each item.

The organizing principle throughout is to prioritize information the customer needs to make a decision over information the company wants to tell them. Customer-centered ordering: what does it do for me, what does it cost, when will I have it, what do others think of it. Company-centered ordering (history, marketing language, certifications) belongs further down or in a separate tab.

## Relationship to adjacent pages

The product details page sits between browsing ([[featured-products]], [[category-pages|category pages]], [[organized-search-results]]) and committing ([[shopping-cart]], [[quick-flow-checkout]]). Its Add to Cart button is the handoff point into the checkout flow. Getting the handoff right — clear button, no ambiguity about what gets added — prevents the confusion that causes customers to abandon between product page and cart.
