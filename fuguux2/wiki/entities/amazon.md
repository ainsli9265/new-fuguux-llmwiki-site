# Amazon.com

An e-commerce site repeatedly cited as a positive example of [[customer-centered-design]]: built "from the ground up" around customer needs; uses a shaded, three-dimensional [[action-buttons|action button]] ("Find Gifts") as an effective call to action; one of the major sites whose core interaction patterns stayed structurally stable from 2001 to 2006 even as visual styling evolved (see [[web-design-patterns]]); and, per [[web-site-development-process]], one of the companies credited with successfully using **virtual testing** — showing a redesign to a small subset of customers and comparing their behavior against everyone else's — before fully switching over to a new site design. Also used in [[organized-search-results]] as the worked example for two search-results behaviors: grouping hits into categories (e.g. "Books" vs. "zShops" for the same query) rather than returning one flat list, and silently correcting a likely misspelling (e.g. "crossing cashm" → "crossing chasm") and showing those results instead of a bare "no results found."

The most heavily-cited worked example in Pattern Group G ("Advanced E-Commerce"), spanning six different patterns: shopping-cart-aware [[cross-selling-and-up-selling|related-product recommendations]] both during checkout and again after it completes; category recommendations in [[personalized-recommendations]] based on past purchases; the full [[recommendation-community]] review-writing flow (write → preview/edit → post) plus a "was this review helpful?" meta-rating system; an address-book pick list for assigning [[multiple-destinations|destinations per item]] within one order; a [[gift-giving|gift-finder]] tool that walks customers through identifying a recipient's interests; and, in [[order-tracking-and-history]], a modifiable order history with real shipment tracking.

Also cited across Pattern Group F ("Basic E-Commerce") for product page and checkout design: the [[clean-product-details|product details page]] hides the Add to Cart button when an item is out of stock and substitutes "Pre-order" for out-of-release items; the [[shopping-cart|shopping cart]] shows high-visibility Add to Cart controls and surfaces cross-sells in-cart; the [[quick-flow-checkout|checkout flow]] provides a guest account path and stored-address selection; and the [[easy-returns|return process]] includes online initiation with a trackable printed label and a post-confirmation cross-sell.
