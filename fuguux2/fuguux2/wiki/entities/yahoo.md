# Yahoo!

A search/portal site used as a recurring example of [[customer-centered-design]] done well despite minimal visual flash: a mostly-text, sparse-graphics interface that loads quickly, attributed to interaction design and usability research being integral to Yahoo!'s development process — customer needs identified via field studies, interviews, and usability evaluations, then designs tailored to match. Also listed, alongside eBay, Amazon, Google, CNN, and MSN, among major sites whose core interaction patterns (navigation, search, checkout) stayed structurally stable from 2001 to 2006 even as visual styling evolved — cited as evidence for the durability of [[web-design-patterns]]. The book's glossary separately names Yahoo! as the best-known example of a **directory** — a hand-curated, hierarchically-organized listing of links to other sites, as opposed to a search engine's algorithmically-ranked results.

Yahoo! Video is the [[recommendation-community]] worked example for keeping review guidelines on the same page as the input form, and for previewing a customer's review exactly as it will appear before letting them post it.
