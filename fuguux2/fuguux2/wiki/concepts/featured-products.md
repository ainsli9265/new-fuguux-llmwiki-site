# Featured Products

**Problem:** a site with tens, hundreds, or millions of products looks bland and tedious if the only ways in are long lists or a search box — customers who like to browse, rather than search for something specific, find nothing to explore.

**Solution:** build category-level pages that highlight a curated subset of products, editorialized rather than presented as a bare name/picture/price list:

- **Editorialize.** A short description with personality (friendly, authoritative, wisecracking — whatever voice the site picks, applied consistently) substitutes for the salesperson a customer would get in a physical store, and makes the recommendation feel less like an algorithm.
- **Offer different kinds of recommendations** for different shopper motivations — "Best-Sellers," "Editors' Picks," "Rare Finds," "New Releases," "On Sale" — since one customer wants what's popular while another wants what's cheap or what's new. Cross-reference what a recommendation is *for* (a product type, a brand, a price point, a gift) against [[category-pages|category pages]], [[search-action-module]], [[gift-giving]], and [[process-funnel|process-funnel-driven]] gift finders to decide which tool fits which need.
- **Provide opportunities to explore** for visitors with no specific goal in mind — highlighting imagery and detail (rather than a dense list) compels browsing-minded customers to click through.

**Forces:** a featured-products list must be easy to update (manually or automatically) or it goes stale fast, which undercuts the "always something new" promise that justifies it in the first place.

Featured products are one of four related but independent ways to provide recommendations on an e-commerce site, alongside [[cross-selling-and-up-selling]], [[personalized-recommendations]], and [[recommendation-community]] — a site can use any or all of them simultaneously, since each answers a different question ("what's worth featuring generally" vs. "what goes with this" vs. "what does this customer specifically want" vs. "what do other customers say"). A customer who clicks through from a featured-products list lands on a [[clean-product-details|product details page]], the decision point where the purchase commitment is made; from there, the item enters the [[shopping-cart|shopping cart]] before checkout.
