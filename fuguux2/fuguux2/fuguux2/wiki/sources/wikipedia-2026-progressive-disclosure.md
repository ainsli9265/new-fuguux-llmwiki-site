# Progressive disclosure — Wikipedia

Short Wikipedia article (effectively a stub) defining progressive disclosure as an interaction design pattern. Adds to the wiki's existing [[progressive-disclosure]] page: the macOS print dialog as the canonical named example ("Show Details" button expanding a collapsed options panel); a physical-world analogy from theme park queue design (only a small segment of a waiting line is visible at a time, reducing perceived wait discouragement); and the explicit framing that information is revealed "when it becomes relevant to the current task" — a task-relevance qualifier that complements the existing page's "on demand" framing.

**References cited:** Jakob Nielsen (Nielsen Norman Group article on progressive disclosure); Donald Norman and Stephen Draper, *User Centered System Design: New Perspectives on Human-Computer Interaction* (1986, L. Erlbaum Associates) — the edited volume in which Norman's early design principles appeared, predating *The Design of Everyday Things*.

**Excluded:** Kristina Hooper Woolsey (individual, Apple Human Interface Group, 1985 — historical attribution, but individuals are out of scope per `purpose.md`); UXPin article (commercial UX tool company, no design substance beyond the pattern definition); "Disclosure widget" and "Chunking (writing)" see-also entries (too thin — single-sentence cross-references with no content added here).
