# Easy Returns

A clear, accessible return process is a trust signal that operates before the purchase decision, not only after a return is needed. Customers who encounter a vague, hard-to-find, or punitive return policy at purchase time are more likely to leave without buying. Customers who can see a clear, fair policy before committing are more likely to convert and more likely to purchase again.

## Policy visibility

The return policy should be linked or summarized on:

- Every product details page, near the price and shipping information — this is where purchase anxiety peaks
- Every checkout page, especially the order summary step where the customer reviews before committing
- Category and search results pages, so browsing customers can see the policy without clicking into a product
- The homepage, as a persistent trust signal

A return policy buried in a footer link or a separate FAQ page works only for customers who know to look — it misses the moment of maximum doubt, which is the product page.

## Online return process

A self-service return funnel removes the friction that makes customers dread returns and keeps them from purchasing in the first place. The flow:

1. **Find the order** — customer locates the purchase in their [[order-tracking-and-history|order history]] or via an order number
2. **Select items to return** — choose which items from that order to return, with quantities if applicable
3. **Select reason** — a short pick list of return reasons (wrong size, not as described, changed mind, damaged/defective); this data is valuable for inventory and product quality feedback
4. **Print return label** — generate a prepaid, trackable shipping label the customer prints and attaches to the package

## Trackable labels

A trackable return label lets the customer verify that the return was received, removing the anxiety of uncertainty ("did they get it?"). It also creates a processing record on the retailer's side. The key trust element is credit speed: the faster a refund or exchange is processed after the return is received, the more likely the customer is to purchase again. Delay at the credit stage undercuts the goodwill of an otherwise smooth return experience.

## Relationship to purchase confidence

Returns and purchases form a loop: a generous, visible return policy increases purchase confidence, which increases orders, which increases the number of returns — but also increases the number of customers who purchase specifically because they trust the return process, which increases repeat purchases. Treating the return policy as a cost center to minimize rather than a trust signal to invest in typically reduces both conversion and lifetime customer value.
