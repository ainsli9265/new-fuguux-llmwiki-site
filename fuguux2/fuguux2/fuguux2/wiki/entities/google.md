# Google

A search site cited as evidence that good [[customer-centered-design]] pays off without heavy marketing spend: it became the dominant search engine with little or no advertising, attributed to simply working better than competing search sites and customers recommending it by word of mouth. Also one of the major sites whose core interaction patterns stayed structurally stable from 2001 to 2006 (see [[web-design-patterns]]), and, per [[web-site-development-process]], one of the companies credited with successfully using **virtual testing** — showing a redesign to a small subset of customers and comparing their behavior against everyone else's — before fully switching over to a new site design. Also held up in [[organized-search-results]] as a model results page: a clean, minimalist layout that repeats the search box at both the top and bottom of the page so customers can refine or restart a search without scrolling back up, and uses an unusually large clickable target for its "Next" pagination link — a design choice the source notes doubles as an accessibility benefit for customers with limited motor control.
