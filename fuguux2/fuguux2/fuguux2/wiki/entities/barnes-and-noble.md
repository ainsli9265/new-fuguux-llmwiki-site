# Barnes & Noble

A bookseller cited across two Pattern Group chapters of *The Design of Sites* as an example of wish list implementation. Barnes & Noble's site allows customers to save items to a persistent wish list separate from the shopping cart — items that the customer is considering or wants others to know about without yet committing to purchase. The site also makes it easy to move items from the wish list directly to the cart, reducing the friction of returning to a considered purchase. Referenced in both Pattern Group G (Advanced E-Commerce) and Pattern Group F (Basic E-Commerce) for this pattern.
